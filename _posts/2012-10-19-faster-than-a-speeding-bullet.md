Yesterday my Retina MacBook Pro finally came in the mail. Needless to say, I&#8217;m very excited, and this machine definitely performs as well as I hoped it would.<figure id="attachment_617" style="width: 584px;" class="wp-caption aligncenter">

[<img class="size-large wp-image-617" title="rMBP Unboxing" src="http://blog.pintozzi.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/IMG_0199-1024x768.jpg" alt="" width="584" height="438" />][1]<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Unboxing, a geek&#8217;s wet dream</figcaption></figure> 

Two two biggest differences are the screen and the flash memory (replacing what used to be a hard drive).<figure id="attachment_619" style="width: 584px;" class="wp-caption aligncenter">

[<img class="size-large wp-image-619" title="Retina Screen" src="http://blog.pintozzi.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/Screen-Shot-2012-10-18-at-9.42.25-PM-1024x640.png" alt="" width="584" height="365" />][2]<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">She packs a ton of pixels</figcaption></figure> 

The amount of pixels blows away competition. 2880&#215;1800 on a 15&#8243; display? Never been done before. And the flash storage crushes other SSDs.<figure id="attachment_620" style="width: 853px;" class="wp-caption aligncenter">

[<img class="size-full wp-image-620" title="13&quot;MBP BlackMagic" src="http://blog.pintozzi.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/Screen-Shot-2012-10-18-at-10.38.40-PM.png" alt="" width="853" height="878" />][3]<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">13&#8243; MacBook Pro with a 64GB OWC SSD</figcaption></figure> <figure id="attachment_621" style="width: 853px;" class="wp-caption aligncenter">[<img class="size-full wp-image-621" title="Hackintosh BlackMagic" src="http://blog.pintozzi.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/Screen-Shot-2012-10-18-at-9.11.12-PM.png" alt="" width="853" height="878" />][4]<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">i7 Hackintosh with a 128GB Crucial M4 SSD</figcaption></figure> <figure id="attachment_622" style="width: 584px;" class="wp-caption aligncenter">[<img class="size-large wp-image-622" title="rMBP BlackMagic" src="http://blog.pintozzi.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/Screen-Shot-2012-10-18-at-5.18.40-PM-994x1024.png" alt="" width="584" height="601" />][5]<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">15&#8243; Retina MacBook Pro with &#8220;Flash Memory&#8221;</figcaption></figure> 

Boot times simply cease to exist. EFI takes longer to initialize than it does for the OS to load.

Obviously CPU is a big factor as well. I paid the extra $100 for an upgrade from 2.3 GHz to 2.6GHz and it&#8217;s noticeably snappier. While it doesn&#8217;t crush an overclocked Hackintosh for speed, [it definitely keeps up][6] (which is impressive as it&#8217;s over 1.1GHz slower!).
