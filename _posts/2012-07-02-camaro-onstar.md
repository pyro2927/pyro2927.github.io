On Friday I was lucky enough to be able to purchase a 2012 45th Anniversary Edition 2SS Camaro.  It has been a blast to drive, and I&#8217;ve been learning more and more about it as I drive it.[<img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-426" title="Camaro" src="http://blog.pintozzi.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/2EeNW.jpg" alt="" width="960" height="541" />][1]

[<img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-427" title="Camaro" src="http://blog.pintozzi.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/1OIBp-1024x577.jpg" alt="" width="584" height="329" />][2]

After picking it up in Joliet, IL, I drove straight up to [Wolf River Campgrounds][3]in New London, WI to go camping with Erin and a few other friends.  By the end of the weekend I logged over 400 miles.<figure id="attachment_428" style="width: 584px;" class="wp-caption aligncenter">

[<img class="size-large wp-image-428" title="Wheels" src="http://blog.pintozzi.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/SZVJt-1024x577.jpg" alt="" width="584" height="329" />][4]<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Limited Edition Rims</figcaption></figure> 

It&#8217;s definitely a powerful machine.<figure id="attachment_429" style="width: 584px;" class="wp-caption aligncenter">

[<img class="size-large wp-image-429" title="Engine" src="http://blog.pintozzi.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/OA4JK-1024x577.jpg" alt="" width="584" height="329" />][5]<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">6.2L LS3 V8</figcaption></figure> <figure id="attachment_430" style="width: 577px;" class="wp-caption aligncenter">[<img class="size-large wp-image-430" title="45th Anniversary Paint Job" src="http://blog.pintozzi.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/u9Q2Z-577x1024.jpg" alt="" width="577" height="1024" />][6]<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">45th Ann. paint job</figcaption></figure> 

One cool part is that it comes with a free subscription to OnStar for 6 months.  OnStar integrates nicely with an iPhone/Android app that allows you to monitor your vehicle stats from wherever you are at.<figure id="attachment_431" style="width: 640px;" class="wp-caption aligncenter">

[<img class="size-full wp-image-431" title="OnStar App" src="http://blog.pintozzi.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/Photo-Jul-02-10-51-17-AM.png" alt="" width="640" height="960" />][7]<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Look at those sweet sweet stats</figcaption></figure> <figure id="attachment_432" style="width: 640px;" class="wp-caption aligncenter">[<img class="size-full wp-image-432" title="OnStar Remote" src="http://blog.pintozzi.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/Photo-Jul-02-10-51-19-AM.png" alt="" width="640" height="960" />][8]<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Lock and Unlock Doors!</figcaption></figure> 

The only downside to the app is that it seems to have trouble sometimes connecting to your vehicle.  When that happens, it COMPLETELY LOGS YOU OUT.  It doesn&#8217;t try again to connect, it just forces you to do a complete login again.  If this were a paid app, I would be pretty frustrated, but I guess I can&#8217;t complain since it&#8217;s free.

Overall the car is awesome.  Driving is tons of fun, and the fact that it&#8217;s a manual transmission causes me to pay much more attention to driving and almost no attention to things like my phone.  Fun AND safe!
